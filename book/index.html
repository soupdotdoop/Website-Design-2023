BATMAN:
The Black Mirror

Written by: Scott Snyder

Art by: Jock and Francesco Francavilla




Summary:

The plot tells the continued adventures of Dick Grayson (the former first Robin and Nightwing) as Batman, as Bruce Wayne continues his work for Batman Incorporated. Dick must battle against several villains, one of whom is selling various super villain weapons and items in an underground black market. Meanwhile, Jim Gordon and his daughter Barbara Gordon must contend with the return of James Gordon Jr., whom they have ex-communicated due to his continued psychopathy. Dick is pushed to his limits in this thrilling mystery.


Genre: Superhero, mystery, thriller


Published: Janurary - October 2011


Rating: 9.5/10 (IGN), 4.3/10 (Goodreads), 4.7/5 (Amazon)

The Characters:

Dick Grayson - Batman


Once the first Robin, later Nightwing, and now has taken the mantle of Batman. Unlike Bruce Wayne, Dick is known for his friendly and empathetic demeanor.

Barbara Gordon - Oracle


Daughter of Commisioner Gordon. Barbara was once Batgirl, until she was paralized from the waist down. Now, she fights crime as Oracle. Her computer skills rival none.

Commissioner Jim Gordon


Commissioner Gordon is one of Batman’s closest friends and one of the few uncorrupt cops in Gotham. While he's remained close with his daughter, Barbara, he hasn't spoken to his son, James, in years. He has doubts about James, but wants to see the best in him.

James Gordon Jr.


Terrible things always seem to happen around James, but no one can ever prove he's had anything to do with them. His relationship with his sister and father is strained due to his psychopathy. But now he's returned to Gotham city looking for a fresh start.




My Thoughts:

I absolutely love this book. The stories build on each other and share many thematic elements and parallels. Dick Grayson is one of my all-time favorite comic book characters so that definitely hooked me in at first. But everything in this book just works so well. If mysteries, thrillers, and or horror comics are your thing, then this is perfect. I have trouble explaining why this story is so good/I enjoy it so much, so I’ll try to break it down.


1. The Writing

Scott Snyder is a very talented writer, and it’s amazing that this is his writing debut at DC Comics. Snyder is extremely talented at crafting atmosphere for his stories, and this is no different. The city of Gotham itself feels like a character on its own. We see how Gotham can corrupt heroes and create monsters.

The mystery in Gotham and behind James Gordon Jr. slowly build and kept me on the edge of my seat. The book is divided between Dick Grayson (Batman) and Jim Gordon. With Dick initially battles horrific new villains that are popping up all over Gotham, and Gordon investigates his past and his son.

Speaking of his son, I want to talk about James Gordon Jr.


––––SPOILERS BELOW!!––––

James Gordon is a wonderfully twisted character. While technically, his first appearance was as a baby in Batman Year One in 1987, this story creates a whole new persona for him. I think the decision to make his appearance completely ordinary was perfect. It makes him different from other Gotham villains while also heightening his creepiness. There would be no way to tell a normal person to this psychopath.

He’s cunning, calculating, cold, and incredibly inteligent. As a reader, you are always wondering when he’s going to snap. And when he does snap, it still feels so shocking and horrific for how brutal and violent he truly is.

The uncaring psychopathic James Jr. serves as a perfect foil for the compassionate and empathetic Dick Grayson.


2. The Art

The art in the book is fantastic! My favorite artist on the book being Francavilla. His colors are just so striking and convey the mood so perfectly. He uses lots of oranges, blues, reds, and purples. The color choices are so bold really give this book an identity that sets it apart from other comics. I am conviced that Matt Reeves' red and black color scheme in The Batman was inspired by this story.
